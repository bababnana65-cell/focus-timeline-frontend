# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who need to follow public events, industry shifts, or long-running topics over time. They arrive with a task: understand what changed, when it changed, and which moments matter without reading a scattered news feed.

## Product Purpose

Event Timeline turns a topic into a structured timeline. It helps users follow multiple topics, scan recent progress, identify major turning points, and move from grouped summaries into detailed source-backed events.

## Brand Personality

Calm, editorial, precise. The product should feel like a trustworthy research desk: compact enough for repeated use, warm enough to avoid looking like an enterprise database, and clear enough to support fast comparison.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic news-feed cards, marketing landing-page hero patterns, decorative dashboards, heavy neon/dark-mode treatment, and UI that makes every topic look equally urgent.

## Design Principles

1. Timeline first: make time, density, and major changes easier to scan than generic content cards.
2. Progressive detail: show summaries first, then reveal event detail and source context only when asked.
3. Calm hierarchy: reserve strong color and weight for current selection, major nodes, and recovery actions.
4. Dense but breathable: support real information density without collapsing touch targets or line height.
5. Consistent action vocabulary: follow, refresh, sort, register, and create actions should look related across screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve visible focus states, 44px touch targets, readable Simplified Chinese text, and restrained motion that does not block scanning.
